Built from Personal Passion. Designed for Modern Professionals.

CubLion began long before it became a business.

Growing up, access to professional sports wasn’t always easy. My father, an immigrant physician, worked tirelessly to build a life for our family. Time and resources were limited. Yet sports — the camaraderie, the tension of competition, the shared emotional highs and lows — were always central to who I was.

I played. I watched. I studied the games. And I always knew sports would remain part of my life.

Years later, during medical residency, time became scarce again. But the desire to stay connected to the sports world never left. What started as borrowing tickets from a friend evolved into something bigger: pooling resources, structuring season access intelligently, and learning how to manage premium tickets efficiently.

From Shared Season Access to Structured Platform

What began as a practical solution to attend games became something far more powerful — a platform for connection. Sporting events weren’t just entertainment. They were relationship accelerators. They were networking environments. They were moments that strengthened friendships, client relationships, and professional communities.

And yet, the premium ticket market itself was chaotic — volatile pricing, fragmented supply, and inefficient coordination.

So we built structure around it.

Over several years, we’ve developed a broad network of season ticket holders, partners, and relationships across major professional leagues. Rather than requiring clients to commit to full-season ownership, CubLion provides structured, curated access — event by event.

Why It Matters

CubLion was built quietly, refined through real coordination, real allocation management, and real client relationships. It combines disciplined execution with a lifelong love of sports.

This isn’t about flashy branding. It’s about building systems that work — and scaling them thoughtfully.
